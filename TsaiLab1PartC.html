Chinese Pumpkin Pancakes (南瓜饼 nan gua bing)

(The way my grandmother taught me)

Ingredients

  • 1 bag of glutinous rice flour (aka mochiko flour or sweet rice flour)
  • 1 small pumpkin
  • Red bean paste
  • Cornstarch

Instructions

Step 1: Making the pumkin puree

  1. First, peel and chop up the pumkin into 1-2 inch cubes and remove all seeds.
  2. Steam, mircowave, or pressure cook the pumpkin until soft. (WARNING if you use a micowave, take it out and check on it every 1-2 minutes because it may or may not blow out the microwave (speaking from personal experience))
  3. Mash softened pumpkin with a fork or blend until smooth.

Step 2: Making the dough

  1. Add the glutinous rice flour in increments to form a playdough-like dough. Add more if dough is still wet or sticky.

Step 3: Shaping the pancakes

  1. Grab a ball of dough a little smaller than the size of your palm. (Use cornstarch on hands to help with sticking)
  2. Flatten the dough out into a 1/2inch thick disc.
  3. Make a small indent in the middle of the disc and scoop a spoonful of red bean paste and place it in the middle.
  4. Fold dough together and close the edges.
  5. Reflatten into a disc and lightly coat with some cornstarch to prevent sticking.

Step 3: Frying the pancakes

  1. Heat up some oil or butter in a pan.
  2. Carefully place the pancakes into the pan.
  3. Fry until bottom is golden brown and then flip and repeat.

~Nan gua bing is best served fresh while it's still warm. When they cool they get hard and don't taste as good.~

ENJOY !
